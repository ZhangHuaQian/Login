China, there is no shortage of people carrying heavy burdens

I thought more than once: why is the Chinese nation the Chinese nation?Why is every Chinese patriotic sentence always engraved with pride?national Inheritance is important, but I don't understand it.I don't want to talk about qu yuan, to talk about every stroke in the history of Chinese culture Something proud of the Chinese nation.

In the summer and autumn of 1998, there was no romantic rain to show us.They fall on the earth, not to nourish the earth, is a warning to the Chinese people, the test of the Chinese nation.

On, from the end of June nenjiang has begun.The nightmare for the Yangtze river basin began in early July.With just a few pages in my hand, with just two hands, I could never, ever paint a nightmare of three months.However, this is not far from the recent history, I only want to look up only to peep into the words.

Yangtze river, the mother of the Chinese, gave her children a severe test that year.She wanted to see if the Chinese nation still existed and the unyielding people still existed. "The main dike of the Yangtze river had eight flood peaks, and the water level exceeded the warning line for more than 60 days."I looked at the cold, heavy figures and tried my best to imagine what it was like -- because I don't believe in documentaries, real moments of crisis are not captured on camera.

The Chinese never knew what it meant to hold back.When the flood came, the party central committee, which was supposed to command and dispatch, suddenly came to the front line. They're here to be the defensive line.No matter how desperate the people were about the flood, they still had to stand there and could not take a step back.

What impressed me most was the speeches by premier wen jiabao and general secretary jiang zemin during the flood relief efforts.Wen said: "when I came here, the general secretary and the premier repeatedly told me that protecting people's lives is the first priority. Where there is life, there is hope."The flood all over the sky, let a person see not a little hope.In mid-august, key party and state leaders all rushed to the front lines to fight floods.Such a short sentence, let me read the determination and courage of the Chinese people.I don't know what the children hiding in the trees thought, I don't know what the people waiting for rescue thought, I don't know what the people like duckweed in the flood.I just don't think Chinese people can be defeated or crushed.

I may not have seen how fierce the flood was, and I don't want to describe it in any way. I think everyone will be silent if I make the matter clear from beginning to end.Will think of the Yangtze river standing on the edge of life and death CARDS, will think of the 360,000 full of blood life, will think of the 12,000 more than 500 cars, will think of the more than 1,170 ships, recall the more than 2,200 aircraft.They carry the mission of each and every son and daughter of China and touch the hearts of every Chinese people.

These Numbers are lovely and warm compared to the cool water level.Somewhere, after the flood, the bodies of soldiers and bright life jackets were floating on the water.The head of the army asked journalists not to film.The general on the levee was in tears and all the journalists were crying with their cameras down."I don't know how many unsung heroes would have risked their lives to fight for this country in addition to 4,150 lives."China, there is no shortage of people carrying heavy burdens."

I can't find any reason not to love the country I live in and the people I live in.

Happened to see a question: if lu xun in modern life, he will write what kind of text ? The answer dawned on me.Mr. A should pick up his medical books and work up his spirits again.If someone said to him, "you look like lu xun!"You should smile and say without looking back: "now China does not need a lu xun."Since the ancient sages put culture into our hands, we can't lose it.At the very least, talk to the people around you, talk to the next generation about where they came from.
